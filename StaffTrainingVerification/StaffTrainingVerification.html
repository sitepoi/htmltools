Your Safety at Work

Your health and safety matter. BC law gives you rights and your employer responsibilities. Here is what you need to know.

Your Three Worker Rights

  1. Right to Know — You have the right to know about hazards in your workplace and how to protect yourself.
  2. Right to Participate — You can take part in keeping your workplace safe, like joining the safety committee or reporting concerns.
  3. Right to Refuse Unsafe Work — If you believe a task is dangerous to you or others, you can say no. Tell your supervisor right away. You cannot be punished for refusing unsafe work.

Reporting Hazards and Injuries

If you see something unsafe, tell your supervisor immediately. If you get hurt at work, no matter how small it seems:

  • Tell your supervisor right away
  • Get first aid if you need it
  • Fill out an incident report form

Reporting is not about getting anyone in trouble — it is about preventing the next injury.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Your supervisor will tell you what PPE you need for your role. Common PPE includes safety glasses, steel-toed boots, gloves, high-visibility vests, and hard hats. You must wear the PPE required for your tasks — no exceptions.

What You Need to Know

  • Where the first aid kit is kept
  • Where fire extinguishers are located
  • Where emergency exits are (know at least two)
  • Where the eyewash station is (if applicable)
  • Where the assembly point is (the meeting spot outside after evacuation)

No alcohol or drugs are allowed on the premises. No phone use while operating equipment or machinery.

Respect at Work

Everyone has the right to a workplace free from harassment and discrimination. BC law protects you.

What is Harassment?

Harassment is any unwanted behaviour that hurts, humiliates, or intimidates someone. It includes:

  • Verbal abuse, yelling, or insults
  • Offensive jokes — including "just joking" about someone's background, appearance, or identity
  • Unwanted physical contact — touching, pushing, blocking someone's way
  • Sexual comments, gestures, or advances
  • Cyberbullying — nasty messages, posts, or sharing photos without consent
  • Deliberately excluding or isolating a coworker

What is Discrimination?

Treating someone unfairly because of who they are. Protected grounds include: race, gender, age, religion, disability, sexual orientation, Indigenous identity, and more.

How to Report

If you experience or witness harassment:

  1. Tell your supervisor. They must take your report seriously.
  2. If your supervisor is the problem, go to HR or the next level of management.
  3. You are protected from retaliation. It is illegal to punish you for reporting in good faith.

Bystander Responsibility

If you see harassment happening, you have a responsibility to speak up. You can:

  • Tell the person their behaviour is not okay (if safe to do so)
  • Support the person being targeted — ask if they are okay
  • Report what you saw to a supervisor or HR

Silence makes it worse. Speaking up makes the workplace safer for everyone.

Investigation Timeline

Reports are investigated within 5 business days. All investigations are confidential. Your privacy is protected.

Know What to Do in an Emergency

Emergencies are rare, but being prepared saves lives. Know these procedures.

Fire

  1. Activate the fire alarm pull station if you discover a fire
  2. Call 911
  3. Evacuate using the nearest safe exit
  4. Do NOT use elevators — always use stairs
  5. Go to the assembly point
  6. Do not re-enter the building until emergency officials say it is safe

Medical Emergency

  1. Call 911 immediately
  2. Send someone to meet the paramedics at the entrance
  3. Get the first aid kit
  4. Only give first aid if you are trained to do so
  5. Notify your supervisor as soon as possible

Lockdown / Security Threat

Use the Run — Hide — Fight framework:

  • Run: If there is a safe path, run. Leave belongings behind. Help others if you can.
  • Hide: If you cannot run, hide. Lock or barricade the door. Turn off lights. Silence your phone.
  • Fight: As a last resort, if your life is in danger, fight back with anything available.

Do not open doors during a lockdown unless you are certain it is safe. Do not post about the situation on social media — this can put people at risk.

Know Your Locations

Make sure you know where these are at your workplace:

  • Fire alarm pull stations
  • Fire extinguishers
  • First aid kit
  • Primary and secondary exits
  • Assembly point (meeting spot outside)
  • Eyewash station (if applicable)

Understanding Hazardous Materials

WHMIS stands for Workplace Hazardous Materials Information System. It is a Canada-wide system for labelling and providing information about hazardous products used at work.

How to Read Labels

Supplier labels appear on products from manufacturers. They include:

  • Product name
  • Hazard symbols (pictograms)
  • Signal words (Danger or Warning)
  • Hazard statements
  • Precautionary statements
  • Supplier information

Workplace labels are used when a product is transferred to a new container. They must have the product name, safe handling information, and a reference to the SDS.

Safety Data Sheets (SDS)

An SDS is a detailed document for each hazardous product. It tells you everything you need to know: what the product is, what hazards it has, how to handle it safely, and what to do in an emergency. Your workplace must keep SDSs where you can easily find them. Ask your supervisor where they are located.

Common Hazard Symbols

🔥 Flame — Flammable. Keep away from heat and sparks.
💀 Skull & Crossbones — Toxic. Can cause serious illness or death if swallowed, inhaled, or absorbed through skin.
🧪 Corrosion — Corrosive. Can burn skin and eyes, or damage metal.
⚠️ Exclamation Mark — Irritant. May cause skin, eye, or respiratory irritation.
💥 Exploding Bomb — Explosive. Can explode if exposed to heat, shock, or friction.

If You Are Exposed

  1. Rinse the affected area with water immediately (15+ minutes for eyes or skin)
  2. Report the incident to your supervisor right away
  3. Seek medical help if needed — bring the SDS or product label with you
